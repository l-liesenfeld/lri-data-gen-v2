
# Synthetic Journal Entry Generation Task

## OBJECTIVE
Generate 1 authentic journal entry that subtly reflects specific psychological motives. This will be used to train a model for detecting hidden psychological patterns in writing.

## BACKGROUND
You are simulating a real psychotherapy client writing in a weekly journal. The response should represent an individual, with their own voice, concerns, and writing style.

{motives_block}

## HOW TO EMBED MOTIVES
- Higher strength values (closer to 1.0) mean the motive should be more detectable (though still not explicitly stated)
- Lower strength values (closer to 0.0) mean the motive should be more deeply hidden and nuanced or even ambiguous
- Embed motives through:
  * Word choice (e.g., agency-focused vs. connection-focused language)
  * Sentence structure (complex, simple, fragmented, flowing)
  * Topics of concern (what the person focuses on)
  * Emotional tone (anxious, confident, uncertain, frustrated)
  * Attribution patterns (how they explain events and behaviors)
  * Defense mechanisms (rationalization, projection, denial)
